Delta

Type: Card
Players: 1
Mathematically optimize pairs of cards to balance your score and stay in the safe zone.

Setup

Remove jacks from a standard 52 card deck and shuffle.
Place one card face up; this forms the operation pile.

Play

Place three cards face up in a row below the operation pile.
Draw four cards into your hand.
You will pair the cards in your hand with the placed cards so that your score stays between 1 and 24 (inclusive), over seven rounds.
The card's face determines its value, where A = 1, Q = 11, and K = 12.

The laid out card of a pair is referred to as the alpha, and the card from your hand as the beta.
If the alpha and the beta are opposite colors, the value of the beta is changed to its mirror value.
To find this, subtract it from 13.
For example: 3 becomes 10, 9 becomes 4, 6 becomes 7.
The absolute difference between the pair's values is then calculated.
For example: αA♣ and βQ♠ make 10, α7♦ and β8♣ make 2.
The sum of all of the pairs' differences is your score for that round.

The fourth card remaining in your hand is placed in the operation pile.
If this card's color matches the previous top card, the current round's operation is addition.
Otherwise, it is subtraction.
Once you have decided on your pairs and operation, modify your total score accordingly.
For the first round, the operation is addition.
For the last round, the operation is subtraction.
Discard the pairs, then place and draw cards as before.

If your score at the end of a round is less than 1 or greater than 24, the game is lost.
If you complete the seventh round in the safe zone, the game is won.
