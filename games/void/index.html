Void

Type: Card
Players: 1+
Mathematically optimize pairs of cards.
This one looks half-assed because I created it and then decided it wasn't interesting enough.
I may attempt to enhance it later on.

Setup

Remove jacks from a standard 52 card deck and shuffle.
Place four cards face up in a row.
Draw four cards into your hand.
To change the difficulty, increase or decrease the number of cards placed and drawn.

Play

You will pair the cards in your hand with the laid out ones in a way that will give you the lowest score.
The card's face determines its value, where aces are ones, queens are elevens, and kings are twelves.
The laid out card of a pair is referred to as the alpha, and the card from your hand as the beta.

If the alpha and the beta are opposite colors, the value of the beta is changed to its mirror value.
To find this, subtract it from 13.
For example: 3 becomes 10, 9 becomes 4, 6 becomes 7.
The absolute difference between the pair's values is then calculated.
For example: alpha ace of clubs and beta 10 of spades makes 9, alpha 7 of diamonds and beta 8 of clubs makes 2.
The sum of all of the pairs' differences is your score for that hand.
Once you have decided on a configuration, add to your total score.
Discard all cards, then place and draw four more cards.
