Conquest is a 2-player memory game.
It is a work in progress.
Last updated: 12/08/2020

Setup

Remove nines and tens from a standard 52 card deck.
Collect court cards into a separate pile, then shuffle both piles.
There is an initially empty discard pile for each draw pile.

Deal

Each player draws one card from the court pile, and five cards from the numeral pile.
The numerals are knights, and the court is the legacy.
Players place their knights in a row, face down.
The legacy is placed below the knights, face down.
These may be looked at by their owner at any time, but cannot be rearranged.

Play

The round begins by both players revealing any one of their own knights for a few seconds.
Players take turns selecting one of their knights to attack a chosen one of their opponent's knights.
Both knights are flipped and victory is determined (refer to the Fight section).
The knight that loses the fight is placed face up in the discard pile, and the winning knight returns to the face down position.

If a player has one knight after losing two fights consecutively, they draw a knight.
If a player has zero knights after a fight, they lose the round and must give their legacy to their opponent, to be placed face up in their trophy pile.
Then, the winner discards their legacy and both players draw a new one.

The game is won by obtaining the majority of trophies.

Fight

If the attacking knight's rank (A-9) is higher and the suit is different, the attacker wins.
If the knights are the same rank or suit, the defending player temporarily reveals their legacy.
If the legacy and the attacking knight's suits match, the defender wins.
Aces are martyrs; if it attacks and loses the fight, the losing player draws a knight.
This effect stacks with the double-loss effect.
