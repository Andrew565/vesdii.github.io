Conquest is a 2-player competitive memory game.
It is a work in progress, so these rules may slightly change later.

- Setup
Remove aces, spade face cards, and diamond face cards from a standard 52 card deck.
Collect face cards into a separate pile, then shuffle both piles.
There is an initially empty discard pile for each draw pile.
One six sided die is needed.

- Deal
Each player draws one card from the face pile, and three cards from the numeral pile.
The numerals are knights, and the face is the legacy.
Players place their knights in a row, face down.
The legacy is placed below the knights, face down.
These may be looked at by their owner at any time, but cannot be rearranged.

- Play
Every turn, a player chooses one of their knights to attack a chosen one of their opponent's knights.
Both knights are flipped and victory is determined.
If the attacking knight's rank is higher and the suit is different, the attacker wins.
If the knights are the same rank or suit, the defending player temporarily reveals their legacy.
If the legacy's color is not the color of the attacking knight, the defender wins.
The knight that loses the conflict is placed face up in the discard pile, and the winning knight returns to where it was, face down again.

Between each turn, both players roll a die once.
If one player gets a higher roll and they have less than three knights, they draw a knight and add it to their row.

If a player has zero knights after a conflict, they lose the battle and must give their legacy to their opponent, to be placed face up in their trophy pile.
Then, the winner discards their legacy and both players draw a new one.

The game is won by having more trophies when the last legacy is discarded.
